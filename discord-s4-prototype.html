Prototype Concept

What We Built & Why

The prototype uses role-first personalization to filter the server into 2–3 relevant zones based on a user's selection. Instead of facing the full channel list, new users experience an interactive, guided tour of the community. The goal is to test whether a structured flow reduces blank-canvas paralysis and the impersonal onboarding experience that currently exists.

Execution: 20 moderated user sessions with participants who joined at least one new server in the past 60 days with a low participation rate. Users drove the prototype without interruption and were allowed to skip at any point.

Three Risks the Prototype Tests

Usability Risk

Will a structured, guided flow introduce unwanted friction for users accustomed to open exploration? Measured via skip rate and at which step users reach for the skip button.

Value Risk

Will personalized guided tours actually move users from passive observer to active participant? Measured via % of users who use the LLM-generated icebreaker vs. write their own.

Viability Risk

Will server admins extract value from the guided tour or opt out? Measured via admin opt-in rate and satisfaction scores among server owners who tested the feature.

Concept Scoring

Top Prototype Ideas

Four prototype concepts were evaluated using a weighted scoring matrix across 10 value propositions (Guided Entry, Reduced Overwhelm, First Post Scaffolding, Social Safety, etc.). Top concepts selected for testing:

IDConceptCore Value Proposition
JMission BoardGamified micro-quest board for the first week — optional, orderable tasks (react, introduce yourself, reply) with visible progress. Reduces "trapped in a tutorial" risk while scaffolding participation.
FInterest-Powered ChannelsUses onboarding interest data to dynamically rank and show only 5 relevant channels instead of 50 alphabetical ones. Progressive disclosure powered by preference data.
TPersonalized WelcomeAt end of onboarding, generates a personalized message explaining which channels match the user's interests and what conversations are happening now.
EAI Training Wheels with Graceful HandoffAI buddy for first 48 hours with prompts and conversation starters. After 3 exchanges, transitions to a real human buddy — so the handoff feels earned, not abrupt.

Key Dependencies

Engineering

Must prioritize the "Zone Tour" logic and the underlying channel recommendation engine for Tier 1.

Trust & Safety

Hard dependency for vetting AI-generated participation prompts to prevent harassment or spam before Tier 3.

Legal

Requires sign-off on cross-server data usage and GDPR/CCPA compliance for personalized recommendations.

Marketing / Community

Must recruit the initial 50 Early Access moderators to validate the opt-in model for Tier 2.
