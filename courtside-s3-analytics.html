03 — Geometric Analytics

Mapping 2D Pixels to 3D Actionable Sports Insights

← 02 Tracking Overview
The Challenge

Translating Pixels to Performance

A camera captures images in a 2D coordinate system. However, sports analytics require real-world metrics like velocity (m/s), distance traveled, and vertical jump height. Without a 3D coordinate system, these metrics remain distorted by camera perspective.

Implementation

Planar Homography Transformation

To solve this, I implemented a planar homography transformation using OpenCV. This maps the 4 corners of the volleyball court from the video frame to a 1:1 scale model of a standard 18m x 9m court.

\[ \begin{bmatrix} X_{world} \\ Y_{world} \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = H \begin{bmatrix} x_{pixel} \\ y_{pixel} \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Where \( H \) is the $3 \times 3$ matrix estimated during the initial calibration phase. This allows the system to calculate the ball's actual velocity regardless of how far it is from the camera lens.

Business Impact

Automated Highlights & ROI

By combining high-accuracy tracking with geometric mapping, the system can autonomously identify "high-intensity events" (e.g., spikes exceeding 80km/h or rallies lasting longer than 15 seconds) and extract them as highlights.
