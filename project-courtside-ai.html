Court-Side AI

Computer Vision for Volleyball Analytics

The Challenge

Volleyball is a high-speed sport with frequent occlusions, rapid player rotations, and a small, fast-moving ball. Existing tracking solutions often failed during "crowded" net plays. The goal was to build a system that could maintain identity persistence for 12+ players simultaneously while processing high-definition 60fps footage without significant latency.

Technical Implementation

Real-time Detection

Optimized YOLOv8 models for specialized sports equipment detection, achieving a mean Average Precision (mAP) of 0.92 on volleyball-specific datasets.

Identity Persistence

Fused ByteTrack motion predictions with Re-ID (Re-identification) features to handle player crossovers, maintaining stable IDs even when players were partially obscured.

Geometric Mapping

Implemented Homography Transformation to map 2D pixel coordinates to a real-world 3D court coordinate system, enabling accurate distance and speed calculations.

The Impact

  • Efficiency: Reduced the time required for post-game highlight extraction by 95% (from 4 hours to under 10 minutes).
  • Scalability: Optimized the backend pipeline to handle 1000+ detection events per second.
  • Accuracy: Achieved 98% accuracy in contact recognition (detecting when a player touches the ball).
