My Story

I am a strategic WFM Leader and UC Berkeley Haas MBA candidate with over 15 years of experience driving large-scale digital transformations. My expertise lies in bridging the gap between complex employment compliance and scalable technical architecture for Global 500 organizations.

At Ross Dress for Less, I lead IT applications for Supply Chain WFM, directing the strategy for systems that power hundreds of locations. Previously, at UKG (Ultimate Kronos Group), I spearheaded cloud migration journeys for over 350 enterprise customers, engineering automated platforms that fundamentally improved operational efficiency and labor transparency.

My professional journey is characterized by leading high-impact workforce initiatives, optimizing HCM operations, and steering end-to-end SDLC to reduce operational risk. I thrive on translating complex technical requirements into actionable product strategies that drive measurable business ROI.

Strategic Philosophy

As a technical lead with an MBA lens, I operate at the intersection of three core principles:

Technological Pragmatism

I believe in choosing tech stacks (Python, Go, SQL) based on their ability to solve specific business bottlenecks—whether that's Go for high-concurrency WFM syncs or YOLO for real-time sports analytics.

Human-Centric WFM

Workforce management systems should respect the humanity of the frontline worker. My goal is to build architectures that ensure fair labor tracking, compliance, and operational predictability.

The ROI of Quality

Technical debt is a business liability. By implementing rigorous quality engineering and CI/CD standards, I reduce long-term operational costs and improve first-pass quality by 20% or more.
