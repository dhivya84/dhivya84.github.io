02 — Relationship Analytics

Using Decay-Based Scoring & Graph Theory to Prioritize Networking

← 01 AI Outreach Overview
The Challenge

The "Dunbar's Number" Problem

It is cognitively impossible to maintain active relationships with more than ~150 people simultaneously. Most networking tools fail because they don't help the user *prioritize*. The goal was to mathematically identify which "weak ties" are in danger of fading and which have the highest latent social capital.

Implementation

Decay-Based Scoring Model

I implemented a relationship strength algorithm that accounts for the natural "fade" of professional connections over time. The score $S$ is modeled as a function of interaction frequency $f$, recency $t$, and the perceived "formality" or depth of interaction $w$.

\[ S = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (w_i \times e^{-\lambda t_i}) \]

Where $\lambda$ represents the decay constant (higher for transactional relationships, lower for mentors) and $t_i$ is the time elapsed since interaction $i$. This ensures the dashboard highlights contacts just as they are about to "churn" from the user's active circle.

Visualization

Graph-Based Network Mapping

Using D3.js, I built an interactive social graph that clusters contacts by industry and interaction density. This allows users to visually identify "structural holes" in their network—areas where they lack diverse connections—providing a strategic roadmap for future outreach.
