Optimizing Vision Models for High-Speed Sports Environments

The Challenge

Detecting the "Impossible"

In volleyball, traditional object detection models fail for three primary reasons:

  • Small Object Scale: The ball often occupies less than 1% of the total pixel area.
  • Motion Blur: At speeds exceeding 100km/h, the ball appears as a streak rather than a sphere.
  • Feature Overlap: Player uniforms, net patterns, and court lines create significant visual noise.
Implementation

YOLOv8 Optimization

To achieve 60fps inference while maintaining high precision, I utilized YOLOv8 with custom training weights focused on sports equipment and human skeletal keypoints.

Key Optimizations:

Augmentation Pipeline

Implemented synthetic motion blur and varying lighting conditions into the training set to improve robust detection in different gym environments.

Tiling Inference

Used overlapping tiling for high-resolution input, ensuring small objects (the ball) are not "downsampled away" during the feature extraction phase.

Result: Achieved a mean Average Precision (mAP) of 0.92, representing a 15% improvement over the baseline pre-trained weights for the ball class.
Technical Depth

Handling the "Ghosting" Effect

When the ball travels at maximum velocity, traditional detection often "misses" frames. I implemented a temporal consistency layer that uses the previous 3 frames to predict the probable detection window in the current frame, significantly reducing "ghosting" or dropped detections.
