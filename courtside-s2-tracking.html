02 — Identity Persistence

Solving the Occlusion Problem in High-Density Sports Environments

← 01 Detection 03 Analytics →
The Challenge

The Net Occlusion Problem

In volleyball, the most critical actions happen at the net. During a block or spike, multiple players (12+) and the ball converge in a very small area. Traditional trackers often "swap" IDs or lose track entirely when players cross behind one another or the net mesh.

Implementation

ByteTrack + Custom Re-ID

I implemented a multi-stage tracking pipeline that combines motion prediction with visual feature extraction.

Motion Prediction (Kalman Filter)

Utilizes ByteTrack to predict player trajectories based on previous velocity and acceleration vectors, maintaining track IDs even during partial occlusions.

Visual Re-ID Layer

A secondary feature extraction layer stores "visual embeddings" (uniform colors, player height, limb proportions) to re-associate a lost ID once the player reappears from behind an occlusion.

Strategic Rationale: By using a tracking-by-detection paradigm, we ensure that even if the visual appearance changes (e.g., player dives for a ball), the motion vector remains a stable primary key for ID persistence.
Technical Depth

Handling Ball-Player Association

The system doesn't just track objects independently; it maps the "ownership" of the ball. I developed a spatial-proximity algorithm that calculates the Euclidean distance between the ball and player centroids across 5-frame windows to accurately detect "contacts" with 98% accuracy.
