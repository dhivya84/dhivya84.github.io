03 — Product Requirements Document

Strategic Pillars, Metrics, Risks & Guardrails

← Research Next: Prototype →
Context & Goal

What We Are Building & Why

This PRD defines product solutions to increase early participation by reducing friction and uncertainty during the initial user experience. Discord's current onboarding tools — rules pages and pinned messages — are static, text-heavy, and passive. New users are met with long rule messages, multiple pinned posts, and dozens of channels, causing information overload before they have taken a single action.

Product Goal: Reduce the 70% churn-in-24-hours rate by increasing the percentage of new users who take at least one action (post their first message) within their first session.

Success Metrics

  • North Star: Average user retention rate on large servers (100+ users)
  • Time to first post after joining
  • Number of posts made within 24 hours of joining
  • 7-day retention rate

Guardrail Metrics

  • Spam or low-quality message rate
  • Moderator workload
  • Opt-out rate from server owners
Pillar 1

Structured Onboarding for First Participation

Goal: Create a clear, time-bound onboarding session (completable in <5 minutes) that familiarizes the user with community basics and guides them toward their first post.

Driver 1: Reduce Time to Value

Help users quickly understand the server structure and identify the 2–3 key channels relevant to them, rather than presenting all 40+ at once.

Driver 2: Reduce Channel Selection Paralysis

Role-first personalization: users select interests to see only relevant "zones" — a guided tour of the server scoped to what matters to them.

Driver 3: Overcome Blank Canvas Anxiety

Pre-populated, LLM-generated icebreaker prompts and suggested first messages that give users a structured script and lower the emotional cost of a first post.

Skip Option

A persistent Skip option at every step. Skip rate is tracked as a guardrail metric to monitor the flexibility-vs-friction tradeoff.

Traction Metrics

  • Onboarding flow completion rate
  • Drop-off rate at the rules agreement screen
  • % of users who click into a recommended starter channel during onboarding

Engagement Metrics

  • Interaction rate with pre-populated icebreaker prompts
  • Time to first post after joining
  • Number of posts within 24 hours

Risks & Counter-Metrics

Standardization may conflict with Discord's sandbox-style flexibility; users may feel trapped in a tutorial. Quality of posts must be monitored against quantity to prevent spam.

  • Skip rate (% of users who bypass structured onboarding)
  • % of server owners who disable the feature
Pillar 2

Newcomer Cohorts for Safe Participation

Goal: New users feel like they are entering a party where everyone already knows each other. Create newcomer experiences — modeled on Midjourney's newbie channels — that make users feel comfortable engaging and eventually become active participants.

Driver 1: Reduce Social Risk & Anxiety

Temporary newcomer-only welcome channels where new users interact with peers who are also joining for the first time — removing the intimidation of high-traffic established channels.

Driver 2: Increase Social Validation

Encourage early positive engagement through reactions, replies, and guided prompts that reinforce participation and help newcomers feel acknowledged and seen.

Driver 3: Participation Rituals

AI-generated, low-stakes introduction prompts and guided first-task activities that help users transition from passive observers to active contributors gradually.

Graduated Transition

A deliberate transition path from the newcomer channel to the main server — not an abrupt cutoff — prevents the anxiety spike that kills participation when the training wheels come off.

Traction Metrics

  • % of new users who join the newcomer cohort channel after entering a server
  • Conversion rate from lurker to participant within the cohort
  • Adoption rate of guided introduction prompts

Engagement Metrics

  • Ratio of newcomer messages that receive at least one positive reaction or reply
  • Frequency of peer-to-peer reactions within the cohort
  • Avg. messages sent while participating in the newcomer cohort

Outcome Metrics

  • % of users who continue contributing in general channels after the cohort expires
  • Total message volume: cohort participants vs. non-participants (lift)
  • User satisfaction scores for new community members

Risks & Counter-Metrics

  • Drop-off rate of users active in the newcomer cohort who go silent after transitioning to main channels
  • Reported harassment incidents within verified newcomer cohorts
  • Moderator opt-out rate from enabling newcomer cohorts
Strategic Prioritization

Why These Two Pillars, In This Order

Optimizing for newcomers — even at the expense of short-term friction for power users — represents a meaningful strategic shift for Discord. Historically, Discord's product velocity has heavily favored community admins, moderators, and power-gamers who demand deep customization.

  1. Structured Onboarding first — addresses the earliest moment of friction. Reducing cognitive overload creates the foundation for engagement before social dynamics even come into play.
  2. Newcomer Cohorts second — once the blank-canvas anxiety is reduced, the focus shifts to social anxiety and early reinforcement through peer interaction.
Execution recommendation: Begin with localized, opt-in experiments on a cohort of medium-sized servers. Demonstrating measurable retention improvements in these environments will build the case for broader platform-wide adoption.
